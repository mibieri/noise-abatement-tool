Welcome

Why this tool?

This tool helps you to calculate the perceived noise level of a road when buildings and noise abatement walls are present. You can draw your situation and compute an estimation of the noise level.

And how does it work?

On the left, you see a box white box with an step-by-step introduction. You also see many info symbols . Just click them to get more information about a menu entry.

Now let's just start with the step-by-step introduction.

Noise Level at Reception Point

Noise Level at Reception Point

The reception point is represented by the yellow dot. The noise level at this position is computed. This could be for example your balcony or a window.

How is it calculated?

The noise level is computed from two components:

  • From each direction (in 1-degree steps), the incoming noise energy is computed and summed up (segmenting approach from Swiss Strassenlärmmodell STL-86).
  • If the reflection model is enabled (Click the yellow reception point -> "Options" -> Reflecion Model), reflections will be added.

Obstacle Effect

Reduction

Obstacles between noise source and reception point will decrease the noise level. The Reduction shows the reduction of the noise level caused by the selected obstacle (wall, building).

Double Obstacle

A double obstacle means that there are two obstacles between noise source and reception point. In that case, the reduction for the double obstacle is computed.

...but be wary!

...the accuracy of those values is limited when more and more obstacles are present.

Reflection Model

Computation of Reflections

Reflections are caused by echoes of buildings and walls. There are various computation models, we currently just use one:

  • Reflexionsmodell gemäss Mitteilung 6, BUWAL 1995: This model was developed to compute reflections in urban canyons. It's only accurate in case of a larg number of buildings close to the road.

Reflection Model

Computation of Reflections

Reflections are caused by echoes of buildings and walls. There are various computation models, we currently just use one:

Yellow Rectangle

Only reflection surfaces inside the yellow rectangle are considered. The reception point is also expected to be inside the rectangle. If the yellow rectangle is only sparsely populated with walls and buildings, the computation will be inaccurate.

Name

Option "Name"

Type

Option "Type"

The type of an object. Can not be changed.

You can add the following types of objects:

  • Reception Points: Position for which you'd like to compute the noise level. Represented by a yellow dot.
  • Road: Source of the noise.
  • Walls and Buildings: You can inculde those objects to calculate the noise reduction.

New Object

New Object

Height of the Reception Point

Option "Height"

Enter the height of your reception point above the road level.

Use a point, and not a comma, as decimal separator.

Obstacle Height

Option "Height"

Maximal height of the obstacle above the road level.

A building with a height of 12 meters that is built 2 meters above the road level will lead to a value of 14 m.

Use a point, and not a comma, as decimal separator.

Noise Map

Noise Map

The noise level in your whole neighborhood will be computed and displayed as a heat map. Move your mouse to see the noise level at a specific point.

When you move an object, the noise map has to be recomputed.

Modern browser and computer is required.

Emited Noise Level

Emited Noise Level LE

Enter the emited noise level LE in decibel (dB). For the canton of Zurich, you find the noise level of many roads on Strassenlärm Informationssystem.

For a road with heavy traffic, one can assume 80-90 decibel (dB).
